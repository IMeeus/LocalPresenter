Breaking Bad was born from a simple but audacious idea. In 2004, writer Vince Gilligan — best known for his work on The X-Files — pitched a show with a single unforgettable logline: What if Mister Chips became Scarface? The concept grew from a dark joke with a colleague about cooking methamphetamine in a motorhome to solve financial problems. Gilligan was fascinated by one question: could television sustain a story where the protagonist deliberately transforms into the villain?
