The reason the corruption is sticky is NPoco's ManagedCache — a process-wide MemoryCache with a one-hour sliding expiration. Once a factory is compiled, it's stored in the cache keyed by type. The broken factory will be served on every request until either the cache expires or the process restarts, which is why a restart fixes it. As for why this is rolling-deploy specific: the race only exists during the very first factory compilation per type. In steady-state operation, after a process has been running long enough to compile all its factories, everything is cached and there's nothing to race on. A fresh deployment receives its first wave of concurrent traffic before any warmup has happened, creating exactly the right conditions for the race.
