Here's the race. Thread A is compiling the factory for AbsenceRequestV3. Thread B is simultaneously compiling the factory for ClockingRemovalRequestV3. Both threads read the current list count — let's say it's five — before either one has called Add. Thread A bakes index five into its IL for the IUser converter. Thread B also bakes index five into its IL for the IWorkflow converter. Then both threads call Add. Whatever lands at index five depends on scheduling. Thread B's factory now permanently applies the wrong converter: it reads an integer from the database, runs it through the IUser converter instead of the IWorkflow converter, and when the generated IL tries to unbox the result to IWorkflow, it throws an InvalidCastException. That broken factory is then cached.
