The recommended fix is Option four: patch NPoco directly in our existing fork. The change is five lines in NPoco's internal method that builds the converter list. We add a private lock object and wrap the two non-atomic steps - reading the current list length and appending the converter - inside a lock block. That makes the read-then-write atomic. The factory index baked into the generated code will now always be correct, regardless of how many threads are compiling factories at the same time. This fix protects every NPoco fetch path in the codebase, not just our FetchMultiple call. Since we already maintain a fork, the distribution story is unchanged - we just add a comment explaining why the patch exists so future maintainers understand it.
